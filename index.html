About Me

I am currently a PhD student at the Institute of Fluid Engineering, Zhejiang University, with an expected graduation date of May 2026. My doctoral research utilizes the Discrete Element Method (DEM) and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) to investigate the mechanical properties of fibrous particulate materials. For instance, I employ machine learning techniques to optimize individual fiber shapes, thereby tuning the yield strength and stiffness of particle packings. Key areas of investigation also include the propagation of stress waves in fibrous materials under low-velocity impact and their flow dynamics during high-speed gas impingement. Currently, I am working on introducing activity into fibrous particles to enable innovative control of self-emergent swarm behaviors in robots without autonomous consciousness, leveraging intrinsic physical mechanisms
